# Verification Patterns

Common manipulation and misinformation patterns to watch for during fact-checking.

## Statistical Manipulation

- **Cherry-picking data** — Selecting only data points that support a claim while ignoring contradicting data
- **Misleading graphs** — Truncated axes, inconsistent scales, or visual distortions that exaggerate trends
- **Correlation vs causation** — Presenting correlation as proof of causal relationship
- **Inappropriate comparisons** — Comparing data from different time periods, populations, or methodologies without adjustment
- **Relative vs absolute numbers** — Using percentages when absolute numbers would tell a different story (or vice versa)
- **Base rate neglect** — Ignoring the underlying probability when presenting risk or change statistics

## Context Removal

- **Quote mining** — Taking statements out of their original context to change meaning
- **Omitting qualifiers** — Removing words like "might", "in some cases", "under certain conditions"
- **Ignoring timeframes** — Presenting time-bound data as if it's permanent or universal
- **Removing statistical caveats** — Dropping confidence intervals, sample sizes, or error margins
- **Selective framing** — Presenting only one side of a nuanced finding

## False Equivalences

- **Treating all sources as equal** — Giving equal weight to a peer-reviewed study and a blog post
- **Both-sidesing settled science** — Presenting fringe views as equivalent to scientific consensus
- **Comparing incomparable things** — Drawing parallels between fundamentally different situations

## Logical Fallacies

- **Ad hominem** — Attacking the person making the claim rather than the claim itself
- **Appeal to authority (improper)** — Citing an expert outside their field of expertise
- **False dichotomy** — Presenting only two options when more exist
- **Slippery slope** — Claiming one event will inevitably lead to extreme consequences without evidence
- **Straw man** — Misrepresenting a position to make it easier to attack
- **Anecdotal evidence** — Using individual stories as proof of a general trend

## Digital-Era Patterns

- **Outdated information presented as current** — Old data or quotes recycled without date context
- **Synthetic media** — AI-generated images, audio, or video presented as real
- **Aggregation bias** — Combining multiple partially-true claims to create a false narrative
- **Platform amplification** — Virality mistaken for credibility
- **Screenshot manipulation** — Edited screenshots of tweets, messages, or articles
