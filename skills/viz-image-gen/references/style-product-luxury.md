# Product / Luxury Photography Style

## When to Use

High-end product launches, luxury brand photography, minimalist commercial photography, cosmetics/perfume bottles, watches, jewelry, tech products on premium surfaces, editorial product features.

## Framework Element Presets

| Element | Preset | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Subject | Single hero product on premium surface (wet marble, brushed concrete, draped silk, dark slate), dramatic shadows defining form, reflections on glossy surfaces | Product is sculpture — treat it as such |
| Framing | Product fills 40-60% of frame, medium macro (showing full product with detail), low angle to elevate product status, slight off-center with negative space for copy | Leave room for editorial context |
| Lighting | Dramatic single key with controlled fill — hard directional creating long shadows, rim light separating from background, subtle gradient on background | Shadows are as important as highlights |
| Mood | Premium, aspirational, quiet confidence, understated luxury, editorial restraint | Less is more — let the product speak |
| Medium | Medium format digital photography (Hasselblad/Phase One look), tack-sharp with medium DOF, clean color science, 100mm macro for small products | Technical perfection expected at this level |
| Style | Minimal commercial photography — Apple product shots, Aesop packaging photography, Diptyque editorial, luxury watch advertising | Premium brands share a visual language |

## Model Recommendation

**True tie** — both excel for different reasons.

**Evidence from shootout:**
- GPT: Superior material rendering accuracy (glass refraction, metal reflection maps, label text clarity), more "technically correct"
- Gemini: Better atmospheric mood, more editorial/artistic interpretation, superior handling of wet surfaces and dramatic shadows
- Both produce professional-quality luxury product photography
- **For catalog/commercial:** GPT (precision matters)
- **For editorial/campaign:** Gemini (mood matters)

## Example Breakdown

**Request:** "Luxury perfume bottle product shot"

```
Subject: Amber glass perfume bottle with gold cap and embossed label, positioned on a slab of wet dark marble, a single water droplet running down the glass surface, gold cap catching a highlight
Framing: Medium macro, low angle (camera at product height), bottle fills 50% of frame with negative space upper-right, slight Dutch angle (2-3 degrees) for dynamism, 4:5 format
Lighting: Single hard key light from upper-left creating dramatic long shadow to the right on marble surface, subtle warm fill from lower-right lifting shadow detail, rim light from directly behind separating bottle from dark background
Mood: Quiet sophistication, evening ritual, intimate luxury — not loud or flashy
Medium: Phase One IQ4 150MP medium format, 120mm macro lens, f/8 for medium depth of field keeping entire bottle sharp while marble blurs at edges, pristine color science
Style: Tom Ford fragrance campaign meets Aesop minimalism — dark, sophisticated, material-focused with editorial negative space
---
Aspect Ratio: 4:5
Model: Gemini (wet marble atmosphere + dramatic shadow handling)
Key Details: Water droplet with refracted light inside it, marble veining visible through wet surface, gold cap reflection showing the studio environment, embossed label text with depth from side lighting
```

**Prompt:** A luxury amber glass perfume bottle with an ornate gold cap sits on a slab of wet dark marble, shot at product height from slight low angle with a subtle 2-3 degree Dutch angle in 4:5 format. The bottle occupies 50% of the frame with editorial negative space in the upper right. A single water droplet runs down the curved glass surface, refracting warm light within its tiny lens. The gold cap catches a precise highlight from the key light while the embossed label text gains dimensional depth from the extreme side angle. A hard key light from upper-left creates a dramatic long shadow stretching across the wet marble surface to the right, revealing natural stone veining through the thin water film. Subtle warm fill from lower-right lifts shadow detail without flattening. A rim light from directly behind separates the bottle's silhouette from the dark gradient background. Shot on Phase One IQ4 medium format with 120mm macro at f/8 — the entire bottle is tack-sharp while the marble surface softens toward the edges. Pristine color science with rich amber tones. The mood is quiet sophisticated luxury — an evening ritual, not a statement. Style references Tom Ford fragrance campaigns crossed with Aesop's material-focused minimalism.

## Known Pitfalls

- "Product photography" without surface specification produces floating-in-void results — always specify the surface material
- Wet surfaces: must specify "thin film of water" not "puddle" — subtlety matters in luxury
- Glass rendering: both models handle well but specify "refraction, internal color, surface reflections" for depth
- Gold/metallic: specify what's REFLECTED in the metal — "reflecting the studio environment" gives realistic results
- Shadow length and direction must be consistent with stated light position — models sometimes contradict
- Text on labels: if legibility matters, use GPT and specify the text explicitly

## Comparison Notes (GPT vs Gemini Shootout)

- **GPT:** More technically precise material rendering — glass physics, metal reflection maps, accurate label text. Slightly clinical feel.
- **Gemini:** More atmospheric, better at the "editorial mood" that luxury campaigns need, superior wet surface rendering with natural light behavior
- **Verdict:** True tie. Use GPT when product accuracy and label readability are priorities. Use Gemini when mood, atmosphere, and editorial feeling are priorities.
