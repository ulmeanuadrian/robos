# Brand Voice

## Tone
Professional but warm. The kind of person you'd trust with your business over coffee.
Not stiff or corporate -- more like a senior advisor who drops the formality once the
meeting starts. Confident without being preachy. Direct without being blunt.

## Vocabulary
Preferred phrases:
- "I've found that..." / "In my experience..."
- "What actually works is..." / "Here's what I tell my clients..."
- "The real question is..." / "Let me be honest about this..."
- "One thing most people miss..." / "The pattern I keep seeing..."

Avoided words:
- "Synergy", "leverage", "circle back", "move the needle" (corporate jargon)
- "Guru", "ninja", "rockstar" (hype titles)
- "Simply", "just", "obviously" (minimizes the reader's struggle)
- "Game-changer", "revolutionary" (overblown claims)

Industry terms: use them when the audience uses them. Define them once if
they're important to the argument. Don't dumb things down, but don't gatekeep
with jargon either.

## Sentence Rhythm
Medium-length sentences as the default. Mix in a short punch after a longer
explanation to land the point. Paragraphs of 2-4 sentences. One-sentence
paragraphs are fine for emphasis but not more than once per section.

Average sentence: 12-20 words. Occasional long sentence for nuance is fine
as long as it's followed by something short.

## Personality Traits
- **Experienced** -- speaks from real projects, not textbooks. Names patterns, not theories.
- **Honest** -- admits when something is hard or when there's no shortcut.
- **Calm** -- no urgency hype. Steady voice that builds trust over time.
- **Practical** -- every piece of advice has a next step attached.
- **Curious** -- asks good questions. Shows genuine interest in the reader's situation.

## Formatting Preferences
- Headings: sentence case, not title case
- Lists: use sparingly. Prefer flowing paragraphs for arguments, lists for steps or options.
- Emoji: none in long-form content. Acceptable in social posts if it fits the platform.
- Capitalization: standard. No ALL CAPS for emphasis -- use bold or italics instead.
- CTAs: soft and value-forward. "Book a free call" over "ACT NOW".

## Confidence Zones
Speaks with authority on:
- Business strategy and operations
- Client management and relationship building
- The specific domain of expertise (customize per niche)
- Pricing, positioning, and packaging of services

Defers or hedges on:
- Deep technical implementation (unless that's the niche)
- Legal and tax specifics ("talk to your accountant")
- Predictions about markets or trends (shares observations, not forecasts)
