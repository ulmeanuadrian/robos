# Audience

## Primary Audience
Small business owners and startup founders at the 1-10 employee stage. They've
gotten past the initial hustle but hit a ceiling -- revenue plateaued, operations
are messy, or growth stalled. They know they need outside help but have been
burned by bad advice before. Most are 30-50 years old, running businesses that
do $200K-$2M in revenue.

They're smart and capable. They built something real. But they're wearing too
many hats and can't see the forest for the trees anymore.

## Secondary Audiences
- **Referral partners**: other consultants, accountants, lawyers who refer clients.
  They need to understand what you do well enough to explain it in one sentence.
- **Past clients**: stay subscribed for ongoing value. Occasionally return for new
  engagements or send referrals.
- **Aspirational peers**: other consultants who follow your content to learn from
  your approach. Not buyers, but amplifiers.

## Pain Points
1. "I've tried everything I can think of and I'm still stuck." -- Overwhelmed by
   options, paralyzed by choice. Reads blogs, watches videos, buys courses, but
   can't turn information into a plan that fits their specific situation.
2. "I hired someone before and it was a waste of money." -- Had a bad experience
   with a freelancer who disappeared, or an agency that delivered cookie-cutter
   work. Trust is low.
3. "I don't have time to figure this out myself." -- Knows the problem exists but
   can't carve out the headspace to solve it while running daily operations.
4. "I don't even know what good looks like." -- Can't evaluate solutions because
   they lack the benchmark. Needs someone to show them what's possible.
5. "Everyone is selling me something." -- Fatigued by pitches. Wants honest
   advice, not another funnel.

## Aspirations
In 1-3 years they want:
- A business that runs without them being the bottleneck for every decision
- Predictable revenue and a pipeline they can count on
- Fewer hours working IN the business, more time working ON it
- Confidence that they're investing in the right things
- A trusted advisor they can call when a new challenge appears

## Content Consumption
- **Where**: LinkedIn (primary), email newsletters, Google search when they have
  a specific problem, occasional podcast during commutes.
- **Formats**: practical articles (800-1500 words), case studies, short video
  (under 5 minutes), email tips.
- **When**: early morning before work starts, lunch breaks, Sunday evening
  planning sessions.
- **Trigger**: they consume content reactively -- something breaks or stalls,
  and they go looking for answers.

## Language & Literacy
Business literate. Comfortable with terms like ROI, pipeline, conversion, margin.
Not technical -- skip the acronyms from specific tools unless you explain them.
Reading level: clear professional English. No academic language, no oversimplified
listicles. They want substance, not fluff.

Budget range: $2,000-$10,000/month for consulting. Price sensitive but understands
value. Will pay premium if they trust the person and see clear ROI potential.

Awareness level: solution-aware (knows consulting/coaching exists) but not
product-aware (doesn't know YOUR specific offering or methodology yet).
