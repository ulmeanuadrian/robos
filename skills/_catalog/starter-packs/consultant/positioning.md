# Positioning

## One-Liner
I help small business owners get unstuck by bringing the outside perspective and
hands-on experience they can't get from courses, content, or figuring it out alone.

## Value Proposition
You get a senior advisor who's done this before -- not someone learning on your
dime. I work with you inside your business, not from a framework deck. The result
is a clear plan you actually execute, not a 50-page strategy document that
collects dust.

What clients pay for:
- Clarity on what to do next (and what to stop doing)
- Accountability to follow through on decisions
- Access to patterns from dozens of similar businesses
- A thinking partner who has no internal politics

## Differentiators
1. **Practitioner, not professor.** Built and run businesses before consulting.
   Advice comes from scars, not slides.
2. **Hands-on, not hands-off.** Don't just advise -- roll up sleeves when needed.
   Attend the team meeting. Review the numbers. Make the introduction.
3. **Solo senior, not junior team.** The person you talk to is the person who
   does the work. No handoff to associates after the sale.
4. **Outcome-priced, not hour-billed.** Pricing is tied to the engagement, not
   the clock. You'll never feel guilty about asking a quick question.
5. **Long-term relationship.** Most clients stay 6-12+ months. This is a
   partnership, not a one-off project.

## Competitor Landscape
- **Big consulting firms** (McKinsey, Deloitte, Accenture): massively overpriced
  for the small business segment, long timelines, junior analysts do the work.
  You're positioned as: same strategic thinking, fraction of the price, direct
  senior access.
- **Freelance consultants**: cheaper but often lack depth. May be great at one
  thing but can't see the whole picture. You're positioned as: broader experience,
  more structured process, proven track record.
- **Online courses and coaches**: scalable but generic. Can't adapt to a specific
  business situation. You're positioned as: personalized and hands-on, not
  one-size-fits-all content.
- **Peer groups** (EO, Vistage, masterminds): good for networking but light on
  implementation. You're positioned as: goes deeper, specific to your business,
  works alongside you not just alongside your peers.

## Category
Independent business consulting -- the space between expensive firms and
generic courses. For owners who need a real partner, not a vendor.

## Proof Points
- Number of businesses worked with (customize: "worked with 40+ small businesses
  across [industries]")
- Client retention rate (customize: "average engagement lasts X months")
- Specific results (customize: "helped [client type] grow from $X to $Y in Z months")
- Testimonials that mention the working relationship, not just results
- Content that demonstrates expertise (articles, talks, podcast appearances)
- Professional background that shows relevant operational experience
