# Brand Voice

## Tone
Personal and authentic. This is a real human talking to other humans --
not a brand, not a company, just someone who's been doing this long enough
to have something worth sharing. Conversational, like sitting across from
a friend who happens to know a lot about your topic.

Vulnerable when it serves the story. Willing to share failures, doubts,
and wrong turns -- not for sympathy, but because that's where the real
lessons live. Confidence comes from being open, not from pretending
everything is perfect.

Anti-corporate on purpose. If something sounds like it could come from
a press release, rewrite it.

## Vocabulary
Preferred phrases:
- "Here's what actually happened..." / "I'll be honest..."
- "What nobody tells you about..." / "The part most people skip..."
- "I tested this so you don't have to..." / "After X months of doing Y..."
- "Hot take:" / "Unpopular opinion:" (use sparingly, when the take is real)
- "Let me break this down..." / "Story time..."

Avoided words:
- "Content" as a verb ("I'm contenting today") -- too meta
- "Crushing it", "killing it", "absolutely smashing it" (empty hype)
- "My community" in a parasocial way -- say "you" or "people who watch/read"
- "Value bomb", "fire content", "insane growth" (bro-marketer speak)
- "Passive income" without immediately qualifying what that actually means

Platform-specific vocabulary:
- YouTube: "in this video", "let me show you", "here's the thing"
- Newsletter: more literary, slightly longer thoughts, personal asides
- Social: punchy, meme-aware, uses platform native language
- Podcast: rambling is fine, tangents add personality, verbal tics are human

## Sentence Rhythm
Varies by platform, but the default is conversational and uneven. Like
how people actually talk. Short thought. Then a longer one that explores
the idea and gives it room to breathe. Then a one-liner to land it.

Paragraphs in written content: 1-3 sentences. White space is your friend.
Nobody wants to read a wall of text on their phone.

In video scripts: write for speech. If it sounds weird read aloud,
rewrite it. Contractions always. Fragments are fine. "Yeah, no" is
a valid sentence.

## Personality Traits
- **Curious** -- genuinely interested in the topic, not performing interest.
  Asks questions the audience is thinking. Goes down rabbit holes and
  brings back something useful.
- **Transparent** -- shares numbers (revenue, views, growth, failures).
  Shows the work, not just the result. Builds in public when possible.
- **Self-aware** -- knows the absurdity of being a "creator" and doesn't
  take themselves too seriously. Can laugh at their own mistakes.
- **Generous** -- gives away the good stuff. Doesn't gatekeep behind
  paywalls unless it's genuinely premium. Default is to share freely.
- **Opinionated** -- has a point of view and states it clearly. Doesn't
  try to please everyone. Disagrees respectfully but directly.

## Formatting Preferences
- Headings: casual, sometimes questions. "Why I stopped doing X" not
  "Cessation of X: A Retrospective."
- Lists: yes for actionable content. Numbered for steps, bulleted for options.
- Emoji: platform-dependent. Heavy on social/YouTube thumbnails, moderate
  in newsletters, minimal in long-form articles.
- Capitalization: sentence case. Strategic caps for emphasis in social ("DO
  NOT do this"). Never in long-form.
- CTAs: natural and low-pressure. "If this was useful, subscribe" or
  "Reply to this email, I actually read them." Not "SMASH THAT LIKE BUTTON."

## Confidence Zones
Speaks with authority on:
- The creator's specific niche/expertise (the main topic)
- The craft of creating content (what works, what doesn't)
- Personal experience and stories (undeniable -- it happened to them)
- Tools and workflows they actually use

Defers or hedges on:
- Topics outside their lane (openly says "I'm not an expert on this, but...")
- Financial advice (shares what they did, not what you should do)
- Platform algorithm specifics (shares observations, not certainties)
- Other creators' situations (doesn't generalize from personal experience)
