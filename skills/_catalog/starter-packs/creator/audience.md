# Audience

## Primary Audience
Aspiring and intermediate creators. They've started making content -- maybe
they have 100-10,000 followers -- and they're trying to figure out how to
grow and eventually make this sustainable. Age 20-35, digital natives,
spend significant time consuming content themselves.

They're not beginners who need to be told to "start a YouTube channel."
They've already started. They need to know what to do next. How to get
better. How to get consistent. How to not burn out.

They see the creator they follow as someone 2-3 steps ahead of them on
the same path. Relatable enough to learn from, successful enough to
trust.

## Secondary Audiences
- **Casual fans**: follow for the content itself, not the meta-creator
  stuff. They're here for the topic (tech reviews, cooking, fitness,
  whatever the niche is). Don't alienate them with too much inside-baseball.
- **Brand partners and sponsors**: evaluate the creator based on audience
  quality, engagement, and professionalism. Content should demonstrate
  all three without being performative about it.
- **Peers**: other creators at a similar level. Potential collaborators,
  cross-promotion partners, and genuine friends in the space.

## Pain Points
1. "The algorithm keeps changing and I can't keep up." -- Feels like
   building on rented land. What worked last month doesn't work now.
   Wants principles that survive platform shifts, not tactics that
   expire in a week.
2. "I'm creating constantly but not growing." -- Posting consistently
   but the numbers are flat. Starting to wonder if the content is the
   problem or the distribution. Needs honest feedback and direction.
3. "I don't know how to make money from this." -- Has an audience but
   no revenue model. Knows about sponsors, courses, merch, and membership
   but doesn't know which fits their situation. Suspicious of "monetize
   your passion" advice from people selling courses about selling courses.
4. "I'm burning out." -- Creating is their job and their hobby and
   they're starting to resent it. Needs permission and practical
   strategies to create sustainably.
5. "I feel like I'm shouting into the void." -- Low engagement despite
   effort. Wants to build a real connection with their audience, not
   just broadcast. Craves comments, replies, and signs that someone
   is actually paying attention.

## Aspirations
In 1-3 years they want:
- Full-time income from creating (or meaningful supplemental income)
- An audience that's engaged and growing without daily grinding
- Creative freedom -- making what they want, not what the algorithm demands
- A reputation in their niche as someone worth paying attention to
- A sustainable rhythm that doesn't require 60-hour weeks

The bigger dream: creative independence. Being their own boss, setting
their own schedule, working on projects they care about, and getting
paid for it.

## Content Consumption
- **Where**: YouTube (primary learning platform), Twitter/X (conversation
  and hot takes), newsletters (deep dives), podcasts (long-form interviews),
  TikTok/Instagram (trends and discoverability), Discord communities.
- **Formats**: 10-20 minute YouTube videos, Twitter threads, weekly
  newsletters, podcast episodes during walks/commutes, short-form
  clips for ideas and trends.
- **When**: throughout the day. Always consuming. Morning scroll, midday
  podcast, evening YouTube. Weekend deep-dives into longer content.
- **Trigger**: seeing another creator succeed (inspiration or jealousy),
  hitting a plateau, launching something new, or burning out.

## Language & Literacy
Internet-native. Comfortable with memes, slang, and platform-specific
language. Knows what "SEO" and "CTR" mean but doesn't talk in acronyms.
Prefers casual, direct language over polished corporate writing.

Reading level: smart but impatient. Hook them in the first line or
they'll scroll past. Respect their time by being concise and specific.

Spending patterns: will pay $0-$50/month for tools, courses, or
communities. Very selective about paid products -- needs strong social
proof from people they trust. Free content builds trust, paid content
delivers transformation.

Awareness level: solution-aware. They know what creators do to grow
and monetize. They're looking for the specific person whose advice
resonates with their situation and style.
