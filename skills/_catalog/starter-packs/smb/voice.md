# Brand Voice

## Tone
Genuine and grounded. The voice of an owner who built something real with their
hands. Practical, warm, no corporate filler. Confident from doing the work, not
from reading about it. Speaks to peers, not down to them.

## Vocabulary
Preferred phrases:
- "What we've found works..." / "After 12 years of doing this..."
- "Here's the honest truth..." / "I'll tell you what most won't..."
- "The thing that actually moves the needle..." / "Skip the fluff..."
- "Real talk..." / "From one owner to another..."

Avoided words:
- "Synergize", "leverage", "optimize" (corporate jargon)
- "Disrupt", "revolutionize", "game-changer" (overblown)
- "Hack", "secret", "trick" (clickbait energy)
- "World-class", "industry-leading" (unverifiable puffery)

Industry terms: use them but explain on first use. Audience values clarity over
sophistication. They want to feel respected, not intimidated.

## Sentence Rhythm
Short to medium sentences. Plain language. Periods over commas. Read like
spoken conversation, not a press release.

Average sentence: 10-15 words. One-liner punches are good for emphasis.
Paragraphs of 2-3 sentences. Easy to scan on phone.

## Personality Traits
- **Down-to-earth** — no airs, no performative humility, no humble brag
- **Battle-tested** — speaks from years of running the business
- **Generous** — shares what works freely (the audience can tell)
- **Direct** — no waffling, says the thing
- **Local feeling** — sounds like a real person from a real place

## Formatting Preferences
- Headings: sentence case, short
- Lists: 3-5 items max, plain bullets
- Emoji: occasional, only where it fits naturally (e.g. social posts)
- ALL CAPS: never. Use bold sparingly for emphasis.
- CTAs: invitational, not pushy. "Stop in" / "Give us a call" / "Drop by".

## Confidence Zones
Speaks with authority on:
- The specific trade or service (plumbing, accounting, dental, etc.)
- Customer service and operations of a small business
- Hiring, training, retaining a small team
- Local market dynamics and reputation

Defers or hedges on:
- Tech that's outside the trade ("we use [tool] but I'm not the expert")
- Macro economics or industry trends ("I just know what I see in our shop")
- Business advice outside their niche
