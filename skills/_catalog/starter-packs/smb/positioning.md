# Positioning

## One-Liner
The local [trade/service] business that does what they say they'll do — on
time, on budget, and with the same care as if it were their own home/business.

## Value Proposition
You get a local team that treats your job like it matters. Not the cheapest,
not the biggest — the one that picks up the phone, shows up when they said they
would, and stands behind the work after the invoice is paid.

What customers pay for:
- Reliability (they show up)
- Quality (the work doesn't need redoing)
- Communication (you know what's happening and when)
- A relationship (next time, you call them, no shopping around)
- Local accountability (you know where to find them)

## Differentiators
1. **Owner-operator mentality.** The owner still cares about every job. Not
   franchised, not corporate. Decisions made on-site, not through a 1-800 number.
2. **Long-term over short-term.** The goal isn't this transaction; it's being
   the provider for the next decade. Reputation > revenue per job.
3. **Honest pricing.** Quote means quote. No surprise charges, no upsells
   you didn't ask for. If the job changes, you're told before the work starts.
4. **Real people, not scripts.** Talk to a person who knows you, not a call
   center. Same crew on the work, not whoever was available.
5. **Community-rooted.** Local sponsorships, local hires, known by name in the
   area. Not a flash-in-the-pan operation.

## Competitor Landscape
- **Big chains / national franchises**: brand recognition + slick marketing,
  but variable quality and impersonal. You're positioned as: same professional
  standard, plus you actually care about the outcome.
- **Cheapest-bid contractors**: lowest price wins jobs, but cut corners and
  disappear when problems show up. You're positioned as: a fair price for
  work that lasts, with someone to call afterwards.
- **Newer / unproven local competitors**: hungry but unpredictable. You're
  positioned as: years of track record, references they can call, established
  in the community.
- **DIY / online tutorials**: free but time-consuming and easy to mess up.
  You're positioned as: do it once, do it right, free up your weekend.

## Category
Local [trade/service] — the space between mass-market chains and unverified
freelancers. For people who want quality + reliability + a relationship.

## Proof Points
- Years in business (customize: "Family-owned since [year]")
- Number of jobs completed locally (customize: "Over [N] [trade] jobs in [city]")
- Customer reviews + ratings (Google, Yelp, Facebook — quantify)
- Repeat customer rate (customize: "[N]% of our work is repeat or referral")
- Specific guarantees (workmanship warranty, response time, etc.)
- Recognizable local presence (sponsorships, vehicles, signage)
- Real photos of real work, real team members on the website
