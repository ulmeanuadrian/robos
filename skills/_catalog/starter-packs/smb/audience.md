# Audience

## Primary Audience
Local customers and businesses in a defined geography (city, region, or service
radius). Mix of repeat customers, referrals, and discovery-stage prospects.
Their relationship with the brand is built on trust, reliability, and
proximity — not on cleverness or scale.

For B2B SMBs (service or trade businesses): other small business owners who
need the service done right, on time, without drama.
For B2C SMBs (retail, dining, personal services): individuals and households
who value local + quality + a good relationship over the cheapest option.

## Secondary Audiences
- **Referral sources**: existing customers who recommend, complementary local
  businesses (a roofer referring an electrician), professional networks.
- **Hiring pool**: potential employees in the area who might join the team —
  brand voice matters for recruiting, not just sales.
- **Local press / community**: neighborhood newsletters, Chamber of Commerce,
  local Facebook groups — amplifiers when the brand shows up authentically.

## Pain Points
1. "I called three places and only one called me back." — Frustration with
   unprofessional service. Wants someone who shows up when they say they will.
2. "The last person we used did a cheap job that cost more to fix." — Burned
   by lowball pricing. Now value quality over savings.
3. "I don't know who to trust." — Researching online but skeptical of reviews.
   Wants signals of legitimacy (years in business, real photos, real names).
4. "I don't want to be sold to." — Tired of pushy sales. Wants information,
   then time to decide.
5. "Things keep falling through the cracks." — Small problems pile up because
   nobody owns them. Wants a relationship, not transactions.

## Aspirations
In 1-3 years they want:
- A go-to provider they trust without thinking (no more shopping around)
- Less friction in the parts of life or business they don't want to manage
- A reputation themselves for working with quality people (referral effect)
- Predictability — knowing what they'll get when they call

## Content Consumption
- **Where**: Google search ("plumber near me"), Facebook (local groups, ads),
  Nextdoor, Yelp, word-of-mouth, Instagram (visual trades especially), local
  signage, vehicle wraps.
- **Formats**: short videos (under 60s), photos of real work, customer stories,
  before/after, FAQs answered honestly.
- **When**: when something is broken, planned (e.g. seasonal), or recommended
  by a friend.
- **Trigger**: reactive (problem appeared) or planned (renovation, special
  occasion). Rarely browsing for entertainment.

## Language & Literacy
General population. Some technical curiosity but no specialist knowledge.
Reading level: 6th-8th grade. Avoid jargon unless explaining it.

Budget range: highly variable by service type. Sensitive to surprise costs
(transparent pricing wins). Will pay premium for trust + reliability + quality
even at 20-50% above lowest competitor.

Awareness level: problem-aware (they have a need) → solution-aware (they
know your category) → product-aware (specific brands they're choosing between).
Most decisions happen at the solution-aware stage based on referrals + reviews.
