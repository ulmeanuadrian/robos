# Positioning

## One-Liner
The [category] platform that technical teams choose because the API, the docs,
and the actual product all do what they say — and the bill stays predictable
as you scale.

## Value Proposition
You get a tool built by people who've actually run [category problem] in
production. The trade-offs are documented. The API surface is small and
stable. The pricing scales linearly with your usage, not exponentially. And
when you need to migrate, you can — there's no vendor lock-in tax baked into
the architecture.

What customers pay for:
- A working integration in 30 minutes, not 3 days
- Technical support that responds with technical answers (not script-readers)
- A roadmap they can see and influence
- Reliability they can put on their own status page
- Pricing they can predict at 10x scale

## Differentiators
1. **Builder-friendly first.** Onboarding is "read 3 pages of docs, copy a
   curl, see it work." No demo gating. No SDR call before you can try.
2. **Honest limits.** Documents what we don't do, not just what we do. If your
   use case is outside our scope, we'll tell you in the first 5 minutes.
3. **Stable APIs.** Breaking changes are versioned and announced 90 days out
   with migration paths. We don't ship "experimental" features into the main
   API surface.
4. **Predictable pricing.** Linear, transparent, and no "contact us" tier
   gating common features. Costs scale with value, not with company size.
5. **Real engineers in support.** First-line support is engineering, not a
   tier-1 script-reader. Time-to-resolution measured in hours, not days.
6. **Open architecture.** Self-hostable / exportable / replaceable — escape
   hatches built in. Confidence comes from you NOT being trapped.

## Competitor Landscape
- **Incumbent enterprise vendor**: has the brand, has the sales force, but the
  product feels like it was designed in 2015. You're positioned as: modern
  architecture, faster integration, no procurement overhead.
- **Bigger, well-funded startup**: feature-rich on paper but unstable in
  practice + aggressive sales. You're positioned as: fewer features done well,
  technically deeper, no high-pressure motion.
- **Open-source alternative**: free up-front but expensive to operate. You're
  positioned as: takes the operational burden off your team, with the option
  to self-host if you outgrow us.
- **Build-it-yourself**: the eternal alternative. You're positioned as: we
  shipped the boring 80% so your team can focus on your 20%. Honest about
  when build-vs-buy makes sense.

## Category
Developer-first [category] platform. For technical teams who want a tool that
respects their time, their stack, and their long-term flexibility.
