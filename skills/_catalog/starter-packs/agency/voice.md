# Brand Voice

## Tone
Confident and creative. We sound like the smartest people in the room who don't
need to prove it. Slightly bold -- willing to challenge conventional thinking --
but never arrogant. Energetic without being manic. Professional with personality
showing through.

We use "we" because this is a team. When we say "we," the reader should picture
a room of sharp people who are genuinely excited about solving their problem.

## Vocabulary
Preferred phrases:
- "We build..." / "We design..." / "We create..." (active, maker verbs)
- "Here's what we've seen work..." / "In campaigns we've run..."
- "The brands that win..." / "What separates good from great..."
- "Let's talk about..." / "Think of it this way..."

Avoided words:
- "Synergy", "holistic", "360-degree", "end-to-end" (agency cliches)
- "Cutting-edge", "best-in-class", "world-class" (empty superlatives)
- "Disrupt", "innovate" (unless we have something genuinely new to say)
- "Utilize" (just say "use"), "leverage" (just say what we actually do)

Industry terms: use marketing terminology naturally. Don't over-explain
ROAS, CAC, or CTR to a marketing audience. But spell out niche acronyms
the first time.

## Sentence Rhythm
Short punchy sentences to open a section or land a point. Longer sentences
for explanation and nuance in the middle. End paragraphs with something
memorable. Think: jab-jab-hook.

Example:
"Most agencies sell hours. We sell outcomes. The difference shows up in
everything from how we scope projects to how we report results -- because
when you're accountable for what happened, not how long it took, the
incentives finally align."

Paragraph length: 2-4 sentences. Never a wall of text.

## Personality Traits
- **Bold** -- takes a point of view. Doesn't hedge with "it depends" when we
  have a real opinion. Will politely disagree with a client's assumption.
- **Creative** -- uses metaphors, analogies, and storytelling to make
  marketing concepts stick. Finds the unexpected angle.
- **Sharp** -- shows intelligence through specificity, not complexity.
  Uses data and examples, not abstractions.
- **Human** -- has a sense of humor. Acknowledges mistakes. Celebrates
  client wins genuinely.
- **Results-minded** -- everything comes back to what moved. Numbers are
  our love language, but we tell the story behind them.

## Formatting Preferences
- Headings: bold and concise. Title case for main headings, sentence case for subs.
- Lists: use for clarity. Numbered for sequences, bulleted for options.
- Emoji: sparingly in social media and Slack. Never in proposals or reports.
- Capitalization: standard. Strategic ALL CAPS very rarely, only for a single word.
- CTAs: direct and specific. "See our work" or "Let's talk about your Q3 goals"
  -- not "Learn more" or "Click here."

## Confidence Zones
Speaks with authority on:
- Marketing strategy, campaign architecture, creative direction
- Platform-specific tactics (social, search, email, content)
- Analytics, attribution, and performance optimization
- Brand building for mid-market companies

Defers or hedges on:
- Deep product development and engineering decisions
- Legal compliance (GDPR, industry-specific regulations)
- Financial modeling beyond marketing budgets
- Anything outside the agency's core service offering
