# Audience

## Primary Audience
Mid-market companies doing $1M-$50M in annual revenue. They've proven
product-market fit and have real customers, but their marketing hasn't
kept pace with growth. Typically 20-200 employees. The founder or CEO
was doing marketing themselves until recently, or they have a small
internal team (1-3 people) that's stretched thin.

They're past the "figure it out with Canva and willpower" stage. They
need strategic thinking AND execution -- not one or the other.

Decision maker: the CMO, VP of Marketing, or the founder directly.
Often the founder is involved even when there's a marketing lead because
the spend feels personal at this stage.

## Secondary Audiences
- **Internal marketing teams**: they'll work alongside us daily. Need to
  see us as partners, not replacements. Content should make them look
  good, not threatened.
- **Board members and investors**: occasionally review marketing strategy.
  Want to see professionalism, clear metrics, and business thinking.
- **Industry peers**: other agencies and marketers follow the content.
  Good for reputation and partnership opportunities.

## Pain Points
1. "We're growing but our marketing feels random." -- No cohesive strategy.
   Running campaigns because someone saw a competitor do it, not because
   there's a plan. Results are inconsistent.
2. "We can't hire fast enough to cover everything." -- Need a designer, a
   strategist, a content writer, a media buyer. Can't justify or manage
   four new hires. Need a team they can plug in.
3. "Our last agency just sent reports and invoices." -- Burned by an agency
   that was reactive, not proactive. Want a partner who brings ideas, not
   just executes tasks.
4. "I need someone who understands our business, not just marketing." --
   Tired of explaining the basics every time. Want an agency that invests
   in understanding the industry and the customer.
5. "We're spending money but don't know what's working." -- Attribution is
   a mess. Can't connect marketing spend to revenue. Making decisions on
   gut feel instead of data.

## Aspirations
In 1-3 years they want:
- A marketing function that generates predictable, qualified leads
- Brand recognition in their market -- to be the name people think of first
- A marketing partner they trust to run with things and bring ideas
- Clear dashboards that connect spend to outcomes
- The confidence to invest more in marketing because they can see returns

## Content Consumption
- **Where**: LinkedIn (heavily), industry podcasts, marketing newsletters
  (Morning Brew, The Hustle, category-specific ones), conferences 1-2x/year.
- **Formats**: case studies (their favorite -- "show me you've done this before"),
  short LinkedIn posts, thought leadership articles, video testimonials.
- **When**: weekday mornings, between meetings, during commutes.
- **Trigger**: contract renewal season, a competitor doing something visible,
  board meeting where marketing was questioned, a failed campaign.

## Language & Literacy
Marketing literate but not marketing nerds. They understand KPIs, funnels,
and ROI. Don't need hand-holding on basic concepts. But don't drown them
in platform-specific jargon -- they care about outcomes, not which Meta
audience parameter you adjusted.

Reading level: sharp professional. They skim fast. Lead with the headline,
follow with the proof.

Budget range: $5,000-$30,000/month retainer. Willing to start smaller and
scale. Price-conscious but not cheap -- they're evaluating ROI, not cost.

Awareness level: most-aware. They know they need an agency. They're
comparing 3-5 options. Your job is to be the one they trust most.
