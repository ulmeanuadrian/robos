# Audience

## Primary Audience
Online shoppers aged 25-45. They browse mostly on mobile, often during
downtime -- commute, lunch break, evening on the couch. They're comfortable
buying online but not impulsive. They'll read reviews, check a competitor,
maybe leave and come back before purchasing.

They're price-aware but not cheapskate. They'll pay more for quality if
they believe it's real. "Good value" matters more than "lowest price."

Split roughly 60/40 between discovery shoppers (browsing, open to finding
something new) and intent shoppers (searching for something specific,
comparing options).

## Secondary Audiences
- **Gift buyers**: shopping for someone else. Need help choosing. Value
  gift wrapping, easy returns, and "best for..." recommendations.
  Active around holidays and occasions.
- **Repeat customers**: already bought and liked it. Looking for new
  arrivals, restocks, or complementary products. Most profitable segment.
  Respond well to loyalty perks and early access.
- **Wholesale/B2B inquiries**: small businesses wanting to stock or
  resell. Different buying criteria -- MOQ, pricing tiers, reliability.

## Pain Points
1. "There are a million options and I can't tell what's actually good."
   -- Overwhelmed by choice. Doesn't trust sponsored reviews. Looks for
   signals of authenticity: real photos, detailed descriptions, honest
   sizing info.
2. "I've been burned by products that looked great online and arrived
   looking cheap." -- Trust gap between product photos and reality.
   Wants user-generated photos, video reviews, and realistic descriptions.
3. "Shipping takes forever or costs too much." -- Expects free shipping
   over a reasonable threshold. Wants tracking. Gets anxious without
   order updates.
4. "Returning things online is a hassle." -- Needs to see the return
   policy before buying. A clear, generous return policy reduces
   purchase anxiety more than a discount.
5. "I don't know if this will work for me specifically." -- Wants to
   see the product on someone like them. Size guides, use-case examples,
   and "customers like you also bought" recommendations help.

## Aspirations
They want to feel like they made a smart purchase. Not just that they
got a good product -- that they found something others haven't, got
great value, and can feel good about the brand behind it. They want
to tell friends about it. A purchase that reflects their taste and values.

For repeat customers: they want a brand they can trust and default to.
One less decision to make.

## Content Consumption
- **Where**: Instagram (discovery), Google (intent), TikTok (trending
  products), email (deals and new arrivals), YouTube (reviews and
  unboxings). Pinterest for home, fashion, and gift ideas.
- **Formats**: short video (under 60 seconds), user-generated content,
  lifestyle photos, comparison guides, "best of" roundups, email
  with clear product images and one CTA.
- **When**: evenings and weekends for browsing. Weekday mornings for
  intent purchases. Paydays and sale events for committed buying.
- **Trigger**: social media ad, friend's recommendation, Google search,
  email from a brand they already follow, influencer mention.

## Language & Literacy
Consumer-grade language. No industry jargon. Describe the material,
not the manufacturing process. Say "feels soft" not "200-thread-count
percale weave" (unless your audience specifically cares about specs).

Reading level: conversational. Skim-friendly. The first sentence of
any product description needs to answer "what is this and why should
I care?"

Price sensitivity varies by segment:
- Under $30: impulse-friendly. Low friction needed.
- $30-$100: needs 2-3 trust signals (reviews, photos, return policy).
- $100+: needs strong social proof, detailed info, and possibly a
  comparison to justify the price.

Awareness level: problem-aware to solution-aware. They know what they
want (or roughly want). Your job is to convince them THIS is the one.
