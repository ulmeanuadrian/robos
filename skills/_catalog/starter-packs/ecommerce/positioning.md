## Differentiators
Customize 3-5 from this list -- pick the ones that are genuinely true:

1. **Materials/ingredients**: what you use and why it matters.
   (Example: "organic cotton from [region], GOTS certified")
2. **Process**: how it's made differently.
   (Example: "hand-finished by [artisans] in [location]")
3. **Direct-to-consumer**: why cutting out the middleman benefits the buyer.
   (Example: "same factory as [premium brand], 40% less")
4. **Guarantee**: what you stand behind.
   (Example: "lifetime warranty" or "100-day trial, full refund")
5. **Social proof**: what customers say consistently.
   (Example: "4.8 stars across 2,000+ reviews")
6. **Sustainability**: what you do that's real and specific.
   (Example: "plastic-free packaging, carbon-neutral shipping")
7. **Specialization**: what you focus on that generalists don't.
   (Example: "designed for [specific body type/use case/climate]")

## Competitor Landscape
Map your store against these common competitor types:

- **Amazon/marketplace sellers**: cheaper but no brand story, inconsistent
  quality, no relationship. You're positioned as: curated quality with a
  brand you can trust and return to.
- **Big retail brands**: name recognition but mass-market. You're positioned
  as: more specialized, better quality in your specific category, direct
  relationship.
- **Other DTC brands**: similar model. Differentiate on product specifics,
  brand story, customer experience, or niche focus.
- **Local/physical stores**: convenience of touch-and-feel. You counter with:
  better selection, reviews, detailed product info, easy returns.
