# Brand Voice

## Tone
Direct and benefit-focused. Every sentence earns its place by telling the
reader what they get or why they should care. Not pushy or salesy -- more
like a knowledgeable friend who's genuinely excited about the product.
Trustworthy first, persuasive second. Casual but never sloppy.

Second person: always "you" and "your." The customer is the main character.
The brand is the guide, not the hero.

## Vocabulary
Preferred phrases:
- "Made for..." / "Designed to..." / "Built because..."
- "You'll notice the difference..." / "Here's what that means for you..."
- "Real customers, real results..." / "See why X people chose..."
- "Free shipping" / "Ships today" / "30-day returns, no questions"

Avoided words:
- "Cheap" (use "affordable" or "great value")
- "Buy now" as the only CTA -- offer alternatives like "See details"
- "Best" without proof (say "top-rated" or "most popular" with data)
- "Luxury" unless the price actually backs it up
- "Unique" (overused -- describe what actually makes it different)

Product descriptions: lead with the benefit, follow with the feature.
"Keeps your coffee hot for 12 hours (double-wall vacuum insulation)"
not "Double-wall vacuum insulation keeps coffee hot."

## Sentence Rhythm
Short. Especially in product pages and ads. Get to the point fast.

Product descriptions: 1-2 short sentences per feature. Lead with the
strongest benefit. Bullet points for specs.

Email: slightly longer sentences are fine. Conversational, like texting
a friend who asked about the product.

Blog/content: medium length. Tell a story, share the "why" behind the
product, give useful information. This is where personality lives.

Social: platform-native. Punchy captions, strategic line breaks,
hooks in the first line.

## Personality Traits
- **Helpful** -- genuinely wants the customer to find the right product,
  even if it means saying "this might not be for you." Answers the
  question before it's asked.
- **Specific** -- uses numbers, measurements, materials, comparisons.
  "3x more absorbent" beats "super absorbent." Shows, doesn't tell.
- **Honest** -- acknowledges tradeoffs. "It's not the lightest option,
  but it's the most durable." Builds trust by not overselling.
- **Relatable** -- sounds like a person, not a corporation. Uses "we"
  for the brand and "you" for the customer. Shares real customer stories.
- **Confident** -- stands behind the product. Clear return policy.
  No hedging language about quality.

## Formatting Preferences
- Headings: benefit-driven, not feature-driven. "Sleep better tonight"
  not "Premium Memory Foam."
- Lists: yes. Bulleted for features, numbered for how-to content.
- Emoji: yes in social media captions and SMS. Sparingly in email subject
  lines. Never in product descriptions or website copy.
- Capitalization: sentence case for body, title case for product names only.
- CTAs: vary by context. "Add to cart" / "Shop now" / "See it in action"
  / "Get yours" / "Check availability."
- Urgency: use honestly. Real deadlines and real stock levels. Never
  fake scarcity.

## Confidence Zones
Speaks with authority on:
- Product quality, materials, sourcing, and craftsmanship
- How the product fits into the customer's life
- Comparison with alternatives (honest, specific)
- Shipping, returns, and customer support policies

Defers or hedges on:
- Medical, legal, or safety claims (follow regulations strictly)
- Competitor bashing (compare features, don't attack brands)
- Promises about individual results (show social proof instead)
