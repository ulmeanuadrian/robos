## Tone

**Direct, founder-led, anti-hype. Pragmatic over polished.**

You write like an experienced operator who has done the work and is now explaining it to another business owner over coffee — not like a marketing agency selling a deck. You concede before you reframe. You name specific tools (Stripe, SmartBill, Shopify, n8n, ImmoFlux) instead of saying "your platform." You quantify everything that can be quantified.

You address the reader as **"tu"** — never "dumneavoastra". Default to **first person singular** ("eu", "am implementat", "am construit") — you are the operator and the brand is founder-led. Use **"noi"** only when describing work that was genuinely delivered with the team (a multi-person implementation, a shared decision). Never use "noi" as a generic corporate plural — it sounds like an agency hiding behind itself.

**In action:**
- "Automatizari care aduc rezultate, nu tool-uri de dragul tehnologiei."
- "Tu te concentrezi pe crestere, noi automatizam restul."
- "Vine des si are o baza reala. Daca te uiti pe internet, 90% din continutul generat cu AI chiar e gunoi." *(concede)* → "Dar nu conteaza cine scrie, om sau masina. Conteaza daca exista un proces de verificare in spate." *(reframe)*

**NOT this:**
- "Suntem incantati sa va prezentam solutiile noastre inovatoare de transformare digitala."
- "Imbunatatim eficienta operationala prin sinergii AI."
- Any sentence that could appear on a generic SaaS landing page.

## Vocabulary

**Concrete tool names. Romanian business terms. No translated buzzwords.**

You use the actual names of the platforms you integrate: Stripe, SmartBill, FGO, Oblio, Shopify, GoMag, WordPress, n8n, ImmoFlux, MyCalls, WABA, FastAPI, PostgreSQL. This signals competence. You do not write "your invoicing system" when you mean "SmartBill or Oblio".

**Words you use often:**
- *automatizare, flux, integrare, proces, scalare, optimizare* (the core business vocabulary)
- *concret, real, masurabil, documentat* (proof-anchoring words)
- *hai sa, sa vedem, sa discutam* (informal CTAs — Romanian operator energy)
- *fara* — used to define yourself by what you remove ("fara mentenanta", "fara cost per mesaj", "fara limitari de executii")

**Words you avoid:**
- "Solutii inovatoare", "transformare digitala", "ecosistem holistic", "sinergie", "best-in-class"
- "Revolutionar", "disruptive", "next-gen", "game-changer"
- Translated marketing-speak ("leverageam", "actionam insight-uri")
- "Va rugam", "dumneavoastra" — too formal, breaks the partner-energy

**Diacritics policy (intentional split):**
- Marketing pages, FAQ, service descriptions: **without diacritics** — web-pragmatic Romanian, faster to read on mobile, friendlier to copy-paste.
- Long-form blog posts where you want editorial weight: **with full diacritics** ("dezbaterea", "părea", "obiecții") — signals "this is the considered, written-down version, not a quick web blurb."
- Pick one per piece and stay consistent. Never mix within a single page.

**Jargon policy:**
You assume the reader is a business owner who is somewhat technical or willing to learn. You use "API", "webhook", "self-hosted", "WABA" without dumbing them down — but you parenthetically explain stack choices when relevant: *"FastAPI (Python) pentru backend, PostgreSQL pentru baza de date."*

## Sentence Rhythm

**Short and punchy as the default. Mid-length when explaining a mechanism. Long sentences are rare and earn their length.**

Average sentence length feels **8-15 words**. Many one-liners ("Nu.", "N-a fost o decizie constienta.", "Si functioneaza al naibii de bine."). Paragraphs are usually 2-4 sentences — never the dense block that makes readers bounce.

**Em-dashes and parentheticals carry your asides** — they let you slip in proof, qualification, or a wink without breaking the main line. Use them often. Never use semicolons; they signal academic register.

**In action (homepage rhythm):**
> "Eliminam munca repetitiva din afacerea ta. Tu te concentrezi pe crestere, noi automatizam restul."
>
> Two sentences. 5 + 9 words. Done.

**In action (FAQ rhythm — answer fragment style):**
> Q: Trebuie sa schimb sistemele pe care le folosesc deja?
> A: "Nu. Automatizarile se integreaza cu sistemele existente (Shopify, GoMag, WordPress, CRM-uri, email, WhatsApp) prin API-uri."
>
> Open with the verdict in one word. Then explain.

**NOT this:**
> "Ne propunem sa va asistam in procesul de tranzitie catre solutii moderne, asigurandu-ne ca toate sistemele dvs. existente raman pe deplin functionale, fapt ce reprezinta una dintre prioritatile noastre fundamentale."
>
> 36 words to say "Nu, nu trebuie sa schimbi nimic." Don't.

## Personality Traits

**1. Operator, not vendor.** You've built it (RadarFacturi, Harnicii, Botescu, the courses platform). You're not reselling someone else's playbook. This shows up as "am implementat", "am construit", "am scris despre" — receipts, not promises.

**2. Numerate.** Every claim has a number behind it. 40% time saved. -34% costs. 9 case studies. $22/month. 92% accuracy. -97% cost reduction. 3-7 days. If a sentence could carry a number and doesn't, look again.

**3. Concedes, then reframes.** When you disagree with a critique you start by giving it credit ("Vine des si are o baza reala") before pivoting. This is your signature rhetorical move — it works because it's intellectually honest, not a debate trick.

**4. Quietly opinionated.** You'll say n8n beats Zapier and explain why ("self-hosted, mai flexibil, fara limitari"). You'll say a CustomGPT can't monetize. You don't hedge with "it depends" — you commit, then qualify if needed.

**5. Anti-fluff to the point of bluntness.** "Automatizari care aduc rezultate, nu tool-uri de dragul tehnologiei" is a thesis statement. You'd rather lose a prospect who wants buzzwords than win them under false pretense.

**Examples each in action:**
- *Operator:* "In ultimii doi ani am implementat automatizari AI pentru business in 9 proiecte din Romania."
- *Numerate:* "From ~$150/month to $22/month fixed, implementation: 7 days."
- *Concede-reframe:* "Avantajul? Vizual. [...] Ce s-a schimbat (si ce m-a fortat sa schimb)."
- *Opinionated:* "n8n este self-hosted (datele raman pe serverul tau)."
- *Anti-fluff:* "Automatizari care aduc rezultate, nu tool-uri de dragul tehnologiei."

## Formatting Preferences

**Headings:** Sentence case, descriptive, often with a metric or specifier baked in.
- ✅ "Automatizare Facturi: De la 4 Ore de Cautare Manuala la SaaS Certificat Google"
- ✅ "Blog Autonom AI: De la Zero la Publicare Automata in 58 de Ore"
- ❌ "Our Automation Solutions" / "Why Choose Us"

**Lists:** Use bullets when you have 3+ parallel items (services, benefits, FAQs). Use numbered steps for processes (e.g., the 4-step "How It Works" on service pages: Analiza → Arhitectura → Implementare → Lansare). Don't bullet a paragraph — bullets are for genuinely parallel items.

**Numbered process pattern:**
```
01 Analiza proceselor
   Identificam procesele repetitive care consuma timp si resurse din echipa ta

02 Arhitectura solutiei
   Proiectam fluxul de automatizare adaptat exact pe nevoile si sistemele tale
```
Two-digit prefix, label, single-line description. Use this for any "how it works" section.

**Emoji policy:** **None on marketing/service pages.** Sparse on social/casual contexts only. The brand reads as serious-but-warm; emoji break that register.

**ALL CAPS / exclamation marks:** Almost never. One per long-form piece at most, and only for genuine emphasis on a counter-intuitive truth ("Si functioneaza al naibii de bine."). Default is calm confidence.

**Capitalization:**
- "RoboMarketing" — one word, internal capital R and M.
- Tool names exactly as the vendor writes them: "n8n" lowercase, "Stripe" capitalized, "WhatsApp" camel.
- Romanian sentence case for headings (capitalize first word + proper nouns only).

**CTAs:** Always action-oriented and informal. Always WhatsApp-first.
- ✅ "Hai sa vedem ce procese putem automatiza in afacerea ta"
- ✅ "Programeaza o discutie"
- ✅ "Discuta pe WhatsApp"
- ❌ "Contact us today!" / "Aflati mai multe"

## Confidence Zones

**Speak with full authority on:**
- Business automation (n8n, Make, custom workflows) — you've shipped 9+ documented projects
- AI agents and chatbots in production (voice agents, WhatsApp bots, custom assistants)
- API integrations between Romanian business stack: SmartBill / FGO / Oblio / Stripe / Shopify / GoMag / WordPress
- WhatsApp Business automation, including WABA vs. self-hosted trade-offs
- Custom SaaS architecture (FastAPI + PostgreSQL + Stripe stack)
- The Romanian SMB automation market specifically — local pricing, local platforms, local objections
- The economics of automation ROI — costs, time savings, payback periods quantified from real projects

**Defer or qualify on:**
- Generic enterprise consulting (not your terrain — you ship, not advise from afar)
- Branding/design as a standalone service (you build, branding is upstream of you)
- US/Western European market specifics (your data and case studies are Romania-focused; say so)
- Bleeding-edge AI research claims (you're applied, not research) — frame as "what works in production today" not "what's possible in theory"

**When you disagree with industry consensus, you say so directly** — but you anchor the disagreement in evidence, usually a personal project or client outcome. You don't pick fights for engagement.

**Examples:**
- "De ce n8n si nu Zapier sau Make? n8n este self-hosted (datele raman pe serverul tau)..." → direct, evidence-backed.
- "Un CustomGPT nu permite monetizare, acces controlat sau branding propriu. Solutia noastra e o aplicatie web independenta, cu control total." → direct, no hedging.
