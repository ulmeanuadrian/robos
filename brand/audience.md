# Audience — RoboMarketing

> Built 2026-05-05 from: site case studies (signal of past work), full blog read (signal of who Adrian writes to), agentsfactory.club (signal of his course audience), and 4 confirmation answers from Adrian. Mode: hybrid (site research + targeted founder interview). Past clients (e-commerce SMB, solo coaches, real-estate agencies) are documented but **not** the ICP — they're outcome of opportunistic delivery, not the audience the brand is built for.

---

## Primary Audience: The Operator-Peer

**The other automation builder in Romania.** Split roughly evenly between two profiles, addressed as one because they share psychology, language, and buying motion:

- **A. Agency owner / technical agency founder** — runs an automation or digital agency with 2-10 people, has their own clients, hits the ceiling on complex work and needs a partner who can build deeper than they can.
- **B. Solo technical builder / freelance operator** — independent freelancer or technical founder shipping their own SaaS/product, treats automation as a craft, lives in n8n / Make / Zapier / code daily.

Both share: technical fluency, allergy to hype, builder-mindset, and the conviction that owning your infrastructure beats vendor dependency.

---

## Secondary Audience: The Owner-Operator SMB

**The Romanian SMB owner who wants to understand it, not just buy it.** Reads pillar guides on the blog, lands on /servicii/ pages, and is making a *real* choice between (a) hiring Adrian to implement, (b) taking the AgentsFactory €499 course and building it themselves, or (c) waiting another quarter. They're the cash engine for service work and a credible course buyer — but they're not who the brand is *for*. They're who it serves.

## Demographics

### Primary (Operator-Peer)

- **Age:** 28–45
- **Location:** Romania (București, Cluj, Iași, Timișoara, Brașov dominate); some diaspora (UK, DE, NL).
- **Role:** Founder/CEO of a 2-10 person automation/digital agency, OR independent technical operator/freelancer/SaaS builder.
- **Revenue / income:** Agency: 100k–800k EUR/year. Solo: 30k–150k EUR/year.
- **Background:** Self-taught or CS-adjacent (mecatronică, informatică, IT operations); typically 5–15 years of building experience.
- **Stack already in production:** n8n (most), Make / Zapier (some), Python/Node.js (writes when needed), Postgres, OpenAI/Anthropic API, Shopify or GoMag for clients.
- **Confidence:** HIGH on profile shape; MEDIUM on exact revenue bands (inferred, not surveyed).

### Secondary (Owner-Operator SMB)

- **Age:** 35–55
- **Role:** Founder/owner of an SMB — e-commerce (Shopify/GoMag), B2B services, coaching/training, professional services.
- **Team size:** 3–25 people.
- **Revenue:** 200k–3M EUR/year.
- **Technical level:** Curious, not technical. Doesn't write code, but understands "API", "integration", "automation" and can reason about workflows.
- **Confidence:** HIGH — drawn directly from documented case studies.

## Psychographics

### Primary (Operator-Peer)

- **Identity:** "Builder," not "consultant." Sees themselves as a maker who happens to also sell.
- **Belief about industry:** "90% of the AI/automation hype is slop. The 10% that works is concrete, measurable, and boring on the surface."
- **Stance on tools:** Pragmatic — uses what works. Will switch from n8n to writing FastAPI when the workflow builder hits its limits, without ego attachment to either.
- **Stance on craft:** Takes craftsmanship seriously. Cares about clean architecture, observability, sane error handling — not just "did it run once."
- **Stance on AI:** Skeptical of agent maximalism but committed to AI in production. Wants to talk about real failure modes, not magic demos.
- **Community identity:** Recognizes themselves in language like "n8n Creator," "automation engineer," "indie builder," "technical operator." Reads/follows other practitioners over influencers.
- **Confidence:** HIGH.

### Secondary (Owner-Operator SMB)

- **Identity:** "I built this business with my hands; I want to keep ownership of how it runs."
- **Belief about automation:** "It's necessary, but I've been burned by overpromising consultants before — show me the receipts."
- **Stance on tools:** Wants to understand the stack just enough to not be held hostage by a vendor. Hence the appeal of taking a course vs. only buying a service.
- **Confidence:** HIGH.

## Pain Points

### Primary (Operator-Peer)

Ranked by intensity:

1. **"I can build it, but the project is bigger than what I have time for / better at than I am."**
   *Translation:* Agency owner has won a complex client deal but doesn't have the headcount or specialty to deliver it cleanly. Solo builder has a brilliant idea but the implementation tail is 4 weeks of work they don't want to eat. They need a competent partner who won't rebrand the work.
   *Why existing solutions fail:* Other agencies = competitors who'll poach the client. Generic dev shops = don't understand n8n / production AI / Romanian invoicing stack. Big consultancies = wrong scale and wrong economics.

2. **"I'm tired of debating absolute beginners about whether AI is real."**
   They want peer conversations, not 101 explanations. They're starved for content that respects their level.
   *Why existing solutions fail:* Most automation/AI content online is either beginner ("ChatGPT for marketers") or US/Bay-area-specific (irrelevant pricing and tooling for RO).

3. **"The economics of my current model don't scale — I'm trading time for money."**
   Wants to productize, build SaaS on the side, or move from delivery to enablement (courses, frameworks, partnerships) without losing the cash floor.
   *Why existing solutions fail:* Generic "build a SaaS" advice ignores their actual constraints (RO market, client retainers, no spare cycles).

4. **"I can't tell if a vendor / tool / approach actually works in production until I've already burned 2 weeks on it."**
   Wants reality-tested signals from someone who has shipped, not pitch decks.

### Secondary (Owner-Operator SMB)

1. **"My team is drowning in copy-paste between tools."**
   Verbatim signal from blog: "cineva pierde 4 pana la 15 ore pe saptamana pe copy-paste." They feel it as headcount pressure: "should I hire someone, or fix the system?"

2. **"I don't know what automation actually costs and I don't trust the quotes I get."**
   Verbatim signal: "nimeni nu stie cat costa realist sa rezolvi problema, si toata lumea vrea audit gratuit inainte sa semneze." They want price clarity before they engage.

3. **"I've been burned by 'automation consultants' who delivered a Zap that broke in week 3."**
   Trust deficit. They want receipts (case studies with metrics) and proof the thing keeps running.

4. **"Romania is behind, but I can't tell if it's safe to be early or risky to be early."**
   Eurostat anxiety: 5.2% RO companies use AI, 72% have low digital intensity. They don't want to be the cautionary tale or the late mover.

## Aspirations

### Primary (Operator-Peer)

**6–12 month horizon:**
- Have a stable retainer book OR a productized service that doesn't require their hands.
- Be recognized as a credible operator inside the Romanian builder community (peer respect, conference invites, podcast appearances).
- Ship one piece of their own — a SaaS, a course, a framework — that compounds.
- Stop trading hours for euros at the unit-of-work level.

**What they'd brag about to peers:**
- "I just shipped a 6-figure SaaS as a side project."
- "My agency moved from project-based to retainer + IP licensing."
- "I taught 100 people to do what I do, and now we partner instead of compete."

### Secondary (Owner-Operator SMB)

**6–12 month horizon:**
- Reclaim 10–20 hours/week of their own time and their team's time.
- Cut operational cost by 20–40% on the manual workflow they automated.
- Add a revenue line that didn't exist (e.g., 24/7 voice agent capturing leads they were losing overnight).
- Feel less reliant on any single hire — automation as resilience.

## Content Consumption Habits

### Primary (Operator-Peer)

- **Discovery:** **Word-of-mouth and networking events** (confirmed — primary motion). Conference talks, mastermind groups, AgentsFactory community, n8n Creator network, peer LinkedIn comments.
- **Consumption:**
  - LinkedIn: reads peer posts, comments thoughtfully (rarely passive scroll). Active before 9am and ~6pm.
  - Medium / personal blogs: reads long-form technical posts when shared by trusted source.
  - YouTube: watches builder vlogs, n8n tutorials, AI engineering deep-dives.
  - Podcasts: indie builder / AI engineering podcasts during commute.
  - Twitter/X: secondary — follows a few key accounts, doesn't post.
- **Format preferences:** Long-form written > short video. Receipts and code snippets > slides. Specifics > frameworks.
- **What makes them read:** A specific number in the title ("De la 4 ore la 30 minute"), a contrarian take from a credible operator ("De ce folosesc tot mai rar workflow builders"), a case study with real metrics.
- **Confidence:** HIGH on word-of-mouth as primary (Adrian-confirmed); MEDIUM on specific platform habits (inferred).

### Secondary (Owner-Operator SMB)

- **Discovery:** Google search ("automatizare procese business", "factura automata SmartBill"), referral from another business owner, occasionally LinkedIn or business news (Forbes, Bursa coverage).
- **Consumption:** Pillar guides on the blog (long, scannable, with metrics), FAQ pages before deciding, case studies for proof.
- **Format:** Reads guides 14+ minutes long when they're seriously evaluating; bounces from anything that feels like marketing fluff.
- **What makes them read:** Local specificity (RO pricing, RO tools), explicit cost ranges, "here's what we got wrong" sections.
- **Confidence:** HIGH.

## Language & Words They Use

### Primary (Operator-Peer)

**Phrases they'd use to describe their problem (verbatim):**
- "Am clienți în plafon, eu n-am bandwidth."
- "Trebuie să scalez fără să mai bag oameni."
- "Workflow-ul ăsta e prea complex pentru n8n curat."
- "Am ajuns la limita la care un Zap pică prea des."
- "Vreau să am IP-ul meu, nu să tot vând ore."
- "Fac asta de un an și încă nu am găsit pe cineva care să livreze fără să-mi rebranduiască."
- "Am nevoie de un partener tehnic, nu de un freelancer."
- "Cursul ăsta îl iau ca să nu mai fiu dependent de tool-uri."

**Jargon level:** Comfortable with: API, webhook, queue, idempotency, rate limit, prompt engineering, RAG, agent loop, n8n nodes, Make scenarios, FastAPI, OpenAI Assistants, function calling, MCP. Will tune out if you over-explain these.

**Words that lose them:**
- "Sinergie", "transformare digitală", "soluții holistice"
- "AI Agents" used loosely (they immediately filter for charlatans)
- "Future of work" without specifics
- Anything that sounds like a HubSpot-trained salesperson wrote it

### Secondary (Owner-Operator SMB)

**Phrases they'd use:**
- "Pierd ore pe lună pe facturi."
- "Mă omoară WhatsApp-ul."
- "Echipa face manual ce ar trebui să facă un sistem."
- "Vreau să știu cât costă concret, nu propuneri vagi."
- "Am avut un consultant care nu a livrat — vreau receipts."
- "Nu vreau să schimb tot, vreau să adaug peste ce am."

**Jargon level:** Knows API, integration, automation conceptually. Doesn't want to learn vocabulary; wants outcomes.

**Words that lose them:**
- Same as above plus excessive code/dev talk that signals "this isn't for me."

## Buying Triggers

### Primary (Operator-Peer)

Specific events that move them from "interested" to "buying":

1. **They just won a deal too big for them to deliver alone.** They need a partner this week.
2. **A peer mentions Adrian at a networking event or in a private chat.** Word-of-mouth lands the trust; they reach out within 1–7 days.
3. **They're about to launch their own product/service and want to compress the learning curve** — buy the AgentsFactory course or the book.
4. **They publish a piece, get pushback, and Adrian's content nails the rebuttal they needed** — they DM him.
5. **A client of theirs asks for something they can't build** — they refer or partner instead of losing the deal.
6. **End-of-quarter gut check** — "I'm still trading hours for euros" → they go shopping for IP, courses, productization frameworks.

### Secondary (Owner-Operator SMB)

1. **A specific operational failure** — a botched WhatsApp campaign, a missed lead during a holiday, a SmartBill reconciliation nightmare at month-end.
2. **A team member just quit / threatened to** — automation surfaces as alternative to hiring.
3. **Black Friday / sezonul de vârf approaching** — must scale customer comms before volume hits.
4. **A competitor visibly ships an AI feature** — fear of being late.
5. **A friend or business contact recommends Adrian** — same word-of-mouth motion as Primary.
6. **PNRR / EU digitization grants** — concrete budget appears, they go shopping.

## Cross-audience notes

- **Trust motion is identical for both:** word-of-mouth + networking. The brand should never optimize for cold paid lead-gen; it should optimize for being recommendable. Every artifact should be the kind of thing a peer would forward to a peer.
- **The two audiences talk to each other.** An Operator-Peer's SMB clients become Adrian's Secondary audience via referral. Adrian's content nourishes both.
- **One brand voice covers both.** The voice profile (founder-led, operator-not-vendor, numerate, anti-hype, concede-then-reframe) is engineered for the Primary and *is exactly what the Secondary trusts about the Primary*. Don't soften it for the SMB — soften it and you lose both.
